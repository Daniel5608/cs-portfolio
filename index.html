-What I Have Learned

Divs:<div></div>

Learning how to use divs has made coding so much easier because you do not have to use the same code for different elements. Now I can just set a div for a specific part of code and then use css to affect that whole piece of code rather than having to type the same code for every element. (The div needs to wrap around a certain part of code to actually affect it)

  • Divs are useful when wanting to affect a certain part of your code and not the rest of your code
  • They also help to create divisions and variables to give that div and kind of trait you want it to have

Tables:<table> <thead> <tr> <td> </td> </tr> </thead></table>

Tables are useful in multiple ways: they can be used to organize a grocery list, or schedules, and even calanders. However there is one thing that is not good that occurs with tables. They don't always correspond correctly to css.

Paragraph tags:<p></p>

P tags are useful because they organize your code when you look at it, and they can be assigned classes to add attributes to them in css. They are also helpful because they provide page breaks and you don't have to use a break tag every time you want to skip a line for a new p tag.

Images:<img src="">

Image tags are useful because they allow us to bring images on to our page to make them look better and nicer so that it is more appealing to the eye. They can also help when you are describing something and you want to show what that something looks like, you can just insert an image tag.

Links:<a href=""> </a>

Link can help with wanting to give your viewer more information on a certain topic without having to write about it because it already has been written about by another person. You can just enter a link into your webpage, send them to the webpage you want, and there they can read more on the information you want them to.

Headers:<h1> </h1>

Headers are important because they can help with letting your viewers know what you are going to be talking about for your webpage, whether it is your title or subtitle. It can also emphasize the importance of a certain topic on your webpage, and you should make an interesting title to catch your viewer's eye.

Lists:<ul> <li> </li> </ul>

  1. Lists can be used to focus your viewers on one idea at a time.
  2. They can be used to introduce a certain idea before another.
  3. They are helpful when you want a numbered list
  4. There are ordered and unordered lists described by ul or ol in the opening and closing tag
  5. Ul is used for bullet points
  6. Ol is used for numbered lists

Comments:<!--whatever comment you want to put-->

Comments are used to add messages to whoever views the page source, but they are not visible to the person who views the actual webpage. They are mostly left for developers of websites.

Span tags: <span> </span>

Span tags are used like divs except they are an in-line element, so they stay in the same line to id, class or style something. They are even more meaningless than divs and you can add lots and lots of attributes to your text and webpage through spans
